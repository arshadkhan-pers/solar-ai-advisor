What Is Solar Financing?

Solar financing allows homeowners to install rooftop solar systems without paying the full project cost upfront. Instead, the investment can be spread across manageable monthly payments.

Depending on the financing arrangement, monthly electricity bill savings may offset part of the loan repayment, helping improve affordability.

Common Solar Financing Options

Solar Loans

Dedicated financing products designed specifically for rooftop solar installations.

Home Improvement Loans

Some lenders allow rooftop solar projects to be financed through broader home improvement programs.

Bank EMI Programs

Certain financial institutions may offer EMI-based repayment structures for residential solar projects.

Installer Financing

Some solar installers partner with lenders to provide financing options during the purchasing process.

Benefits Of Solar Financing

Lower Initial Investment

Reduce the need for large upfront payments.

Earlier Savings

Begin generating electricity immediately after installation.

Flexible Repayment

Loan tenures can vary depending on lender offerings.

Ownership Benefits

Homeowners generally retain ownership of the rooftop solar asset.

Can Solar Be Installed With Zero Upfront Cost?

Some financing programs allow homeowners to install rooftop solar with little or no upfront payment, subject to lender approval, eligibility criteria and financing terms.

In many cases, monthly electricity savings may help offset a portion of the monthly EMI, improving overall affordability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I finance an entire solar installation?

Financing availability depends on lender policies, eligibility criteria and project requirements.

Will my electricity savings cover my EMI?

Actual results vary depending on electricity usage, financing terms, solar generation and local tariffs.

Can I prepay a solar loan?

Many lenders permit prepayment, although terms and charges may vary.

Does financing affect subsidy eligibility?

Financing and subsidy eligibility are generally separate considerations, subject to applicable program requirements.
