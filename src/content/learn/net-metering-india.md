## What Is Net Metering?

When a solar system generates more electricity than a household is using at that moment, the surplus flows back into the electricity grid. Net metering is the billing mechanism that gives homeowners credit for this exported power.

With a bi-directional meter installed by your DISCOM, your electricity bill reflects the **net difference** between what you consumed from the grid and what you exported. In months where your solar generation is high (typically summer), many households find their bills drop dramatically — sometimes to near zero for the energy component.

## Why Net Metering Matters for Your ROI

Without net metering, any solar electricity you generate beyond your immediate consumption is simply wasted (in a grid-tied system with no battery). Net metering turns that surplus into monetary value, dramatically improving the payback period of your investment.

For a typical Indian household:

- Daytime solar generation often exceeds home consumption (since occupants are often out)
- Evening consumption draws from the grid — but your net metering credits offset this
- The net result: your electricity bill reflects only the difference between your imports and exports

This is why solar ROI calculations that ignore net metering are incomplete — it's a core part of how grid-tied solar actually pays for itself.

## How Net Metering Is Set Up in India

**Step 1: Apply to your DISCOM.** After your solar system is installed, your installer will help you apply for net metering to your DISCOM (electricity distribution company). This involves submitting an application with technical details of your system.

**Step 2: Technical inspection.** A DISCOM engineer inspects the installation to verify it meets grid safety and interconnection requirements.

**Step 3: Bi-directional meter installation.** Your existing electricity meter is replaced with a net meter capable of measuring bi-directional flow. The cost of meter replacement varies by DISCOM.

**Step 4: Commissioning.** Once the meter is installed, your system is officially grid-connected and net metering begins.

**Typical timeline:** The entire process takes two to eight weeks depending on your DISCOM's workload and administrative efficiency. This is often the longest part of the solar installation process after the panels go up.

## Understanding Your Post-Solar Electricity Bill

After net metering is active, your monthly electricity bill will show:

- **Units imported from grid** (what you consumed)
- **Units exported to grid** (your solar surplus)
- **Net units billed** = imports minus exports

If exports exceed imports in a given billing cycle, you will typically have a credit carried forward (rules vary by state) or a near-zero bill for that period.

Note: Fixed charges (meter rental, demand charges, minimum charges) are usually not offset by solar credits and will continue to appear on your bill.

## Net Metering Rules Vary by State

India's electricity sector is regulated by State Electricity Regulatory Commissions (SERCs). Each state sets its own net metering regulations, including:

- Maximum system size eligible for net metering
- Whether credits carry forward monthly or settle annually
- The rate at which annual surplus exports are compensated (some states pay a feed-in tariff for annual net surplus)
- Whether batteries affect net metering eligibility

For the specifics in your state, check your SERC's latest net metering regulations or ask your installer to walk you through what applies to your DISCOM.

## Batteries and Net Metering

Grid-tied solar systems with battery backup are increasingly common but add complexity. Most DISCOMs in India require that battery systems do not export battery-stored power to the grid (only solar-generated surplus can be exported). This distinction matters for your net metering agreement.

If you are considering a hybrid (grid + battery) system, discuss the specific net metering implications with your installer and confirm with your DISCOM before committing to battery capacity.

## Net Metering in Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh operates four separate distribution companies under UPPCL (Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Limited). Net metering applications, technical inspections, and bi-directional meter installations are all handled by your city's specific DISCOM — not by UPPCL centrally.

### MVVNL — Madhyanchal Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited

MVVNL covers central UP including **Lucknow**, **Ayodhya**, and **Raebareli**. Net metering applications for these cities go through MVVNL. After an empanelled installer submits your application, an MVVNL engineer inspects the installation before replacing your meter with a bi-directional net meter.

### PUVVNL — Purvanchal Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited

PUVVNL covers eastern UP including **Prayagraj**, **Varanasi**, and **Pratapgarh**. The net metering process under PUVVNL follows the same five-step sequence — application by empanelled installer, PUVVNL inspection, bi-directional meter installation, and commissioning. Typical timelines are two to six weeks in these cities.

### DVVNL — Dakshinanchal Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited

DVVNL covers southern UP including **Kanpur** and **Agra**. Net metering applications in these cities are processed through DVVNL. The empanelled installer submits the application, DVVNL carries out the technical inspection, and then replaces your existing meter with a net meter.

The same five-step sequence applies — application by empanelled installer, PVVNL inspection, bi-directional meter installation, and commissioning. ### Key Point for UP Homeowners

The DISCOM determines which empanelled installer list applies to your city. Before signing a contract, verify your installer is empanelled with the correct DISCOM for your city at **pmsuryaghar.gov.in**. An installer empanelled with one DISCOM cannot necessarily process net metering through another.
