## What Is the Size of a Solar Panel?

Most modern residential solar panels installed in India are approximately **1.7 to 2.3 metres long** and **1.0 to 1.3 metres wide**, depending on the manufacturer, technology, and wattage rating.

**Typical residential solar panel specifications:**

| Dimension | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Length | 1.7 m – 2.3 m |
| Width | 1.0 m – 1.3 m |
| Area | ~20–30 sq ft per panel |
| Power Output | 450W – 600W+ |

Higher wattage panels are physically slightly larger but generate more electricity per panel — meaning fewer panels needed for the same system size.

## How Many Panels Do You Need?

The number of panels required depends on system size and the wattage of each panel. The table below uses modern 500W panels as a reference.

| System Size | Approx. Panels | Roof Area Required |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1 kW | 2 panels | ~100 sq ft |
| 2 kW | 4 panels | ~200 sq ft |
| 3 kW | 6 panels | ~300 sq ft |
| 5 kW | 10 panels | ~500 sq ft |
| 8 kW | 16 panels | ~800 sq ft |
| 10 kW | 20 panels | ~1,000 sq ft |

*Panel counts are illustrative examples based on modern high-efficiency residential solar panels. Actual counts vary with panel wattage.*

## Roof Space by Home Type

**Small home (1–2 kW)**
100–200 sq ft of shadow-free roof space. Suitable for basic appliances, fans, lights, and a small refrigerator. Typical for 1–2 bedroom flats.

**Medium home (3–5 kW)**
300–500 sq ft of usable roof. The most common range for Indian households running air conditioners, refrigerators, washing machines, and lighting. Eligible for PM Surya Ghar subsidy up to 3 kW at the highest subsidy rate.

**Large home (8–10 kW)**
800–1,000 sq ft of available roof. Suitable for larger families with multiple AC units and heavy electricity use above ₹5,000/month.

## Factors That Affect Roof Space Requirements

Beyond the basic calculation, several factors influence how much usable roof you actually have:

- **Panel wattage** — Higher wattage panels (550W–600W) need marginally more space but reduce total panel count
- **Roof shape and orientation** — South-facing pitches are ideal; L-shaped or fragmented roofs have less contiguous usable space
- **Shading** — Trees, water tanks, staircase enclosures, and neighbouring buildings can reduce usable area significantly
- **Walkway and maintenance clearances** — Installers typically leave 1–2 ft clearance around the panel array
- **Tilt and mounting structure** — Panels on a flat RCC roof need tilt frames, which add spacing between rows to avoid self-shading
