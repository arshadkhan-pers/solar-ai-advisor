## Why Homeowners Choose Rooftop Solar

Rooftop solar can help homeowners reduce electricity bills, improve energy independence, and generate long-term savings. Here are the four main reasons Indian homeowners are making the switch:

**Reduce electricity bills**
Generate your own electricity during daylight hours and reduce how much you draw from the grid. For homes paying ₹3,000–₹10,000/month in electricity, savings can be significant.

**Protection from rising tariffs**
Electricity tariffs in India have increased consistently over the past decade. A solar system locks in a portion of your energy supply at near-zero marginal cost.

**Long-term value**
A quality rooftop system generates electricity for 25+ years. After the payback period (typically 4–7 years), all savings are effectively free.

**Environmental benefits**
Solar electricity reduces reliance on coal-fired grid power — one of the largest sources of carbon emissions in India.

## Is Your Home Suitable for Solar?

Many homes can benefit from rooftop solar, but suitability depends on several factors:

- Shadow-free roof area of at least 100 sq ft (more for larger systems)
- Good sunlight exposure — most of India receives excellent solar irradiance
- Sufficient electricity consumption (systems pay back faster with higher bills)
- Suitable roof structure that can bear panel weight (typically 15–20 kg/m²)
- Grid connection via a DISCOM — required for on-grid systems and net metering
- Long-term ownership plans — solar is a 25-year investment

## Understanding Costs and Savings

**System cost**
The total investment depends on system size, panel brand, inverter quality, installer selection, and your location. Get multiple quotes and compare specifications — not just price.

**Government subsidies**
Under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, homeowners can receive central subsidies of up to ₹78,000 for systems up to 3kW, and ₹94,500 for systems between 3kW and 10kW. See the [full subsidy guide](/learn/pm-surya-ghar-guide.html).

**Financing options**
If upfront cost is a concern, solar loans and EMI programs are available. Monthly electricity savings can offset a portion of the EMI. See our [solar financing guide](/learn/solar-loan-and-financing.html).

**Long-term savings**
After the payback period, the system continues generating electricity for decades — representing substantial cumulative savings as grid tariffs rise.

## Understanding Net Metering

If your system generates more electricity than you use during the day, the surplus is exported to the grid. Under net metering, your DISCOM credits this export against future bills — effectively using the grid as a zero-cost battery.

See the [complete net metering guide](/learn/net-metering-india.html) for how the process works in India.

## Before Choosing an Installer

Selecting the right installer is critical — they determine quality of equipment, installation standards, and after-sales support. Before signing:

- Compare proposals from at least 2–3 installers
- Review equipment specifications (panel brand, inverter type, mounting quality)
- Verify warranty coverage on panels, inverter, and workmanship
- Confirm the installer is MNRE-empanelled (required for PM Surya Ghar subsidy)
- Review financing terms carefully if using EMI
- Confirm after-sales service commitments and response times
- Ask for local references or completed project photos
