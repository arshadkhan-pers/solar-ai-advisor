Net Metering Guide For Indian Homeowners

Net metering is one of the most important benefits of rooftop solar. It allows homeowners to export surplus solar electricity to the grid and receive credit according to applicable utility rules.

What Is Net Metering?

During daylight hours, a rooftop solar system may generate more electricity than the home is currently consuming.

Under net metering arrangements, surplus electricity can be exported to the utility grid through an approved bi-directional meter.

The exported electricity may be credited against future electricity consumption according to applicable utility regulations.

How Net Metering Works

1

Generate

Solar panels produce electricity.

2

Consume

Your home uses available solar power first.

3

Export

Excess electricity flows to the utility grid.

4

Credit

Eligible credits may offset future electricity use.

Benefits Of Net Metering

Better Solar Utilization

Excess daytime generation can still provide value instead of being wasted.

Lower Electricity Bills

Grid credits may help reduce overall electricity expenses.

Improved Solar Economics

Net metering can improve long-term solar returns.

No Battery Required

Many homeowners benefit from net metering without installing batteries.

Who Can Use Net Metering?

  • ✓ Homeowners with rooftop solar systems
  • ✓ Properties connected to the utility grid
  • ✓ Consumers approved by their electricity distribution company
  • ✓ Systems meeting applicable technical standards
  • ✓ Consumers complying with local utility regulations

Net Metering vs Battery Storage

Net Metering

  • ✓ Uses utility grid
  • ✓ Lower initial cost
  • ✓ Suitable for most homes
  • ✓ No battery maintenance

Battery Storage

  • ✓ Stores electricity onsite
  • ✓ Backup during outages (system dependent)
  • ✓ Higher upfront investment
  • ✓ Additional equipment required

Important Considerations

  • ✓ Utility regulations can vary by state and utility provider.
  • ✓ Approval requirements may change over time.
  • ✓ Meter replacement or installation procedures may apply.
  • ✓ Export compensation structures can differ between utilities.
  • ✓ Always verify the latest requirements before installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when my solar system generates more electricity than I use?

Surplus electricity may be exported to the grid through an approved net metering arrangement, subject to utility rules.

Do I need batteries if I have net metering?

Many homeowners use net metering without batteries. The best option depends on individual requirements and local conditions.

Will net metering eliminate my electricity bill?

Results vary depending on system size, electricity usage, export levels and applicable utility policies.

Is net metering available everywhere in India?

Availability and rules vary between electricity distribution companies and state regulations.

Important Disclaimer

Net metering regulations, export compensation mechanisms, approval procedures and technical requirements may change over time. Always verify the latest information with your electricity distribution company and relevant government authorities.
