## Why Installer Choice Is the Most Important Solar Decision

The quality of your solar experience — from installation accuracy to post-commissioning support — depends more on your installer than on the panels themselves. A good installer with average panels will outperform a poor installer with premium equipment. Getting this choice right is worth the extra time.

## Step 1: Start With Empanelment Verification

If you plan to apply for PM Surya Ghar subsidy or set up net metering, your installer must be empanelled by your DISCOM (Electricity Distribution Company). Check empanelment directly at **pmsuryaghar.gov.in** — do not rely on the installer's claim alone.

For installations where subsidy is not a concern (commercial systems, off-grid systems), empanelment is less critical but DISCOM registration still helps for smoother net metering approvals.

## Step 2: Request a Detailed Written Quotation

A serious installer will provide a formal written quotation that includes:

- **Specific panel brand and model** with rated wattage per panel
- **Inverter brand and model** (on-grid, hybrid, or off-grid)
- **Total system capacity** in kWp
- **Estimated annual generation** in kWh
- **Complete cost breakdown** — panels, inverter, mounting structure, wiring, installation, GST
- **Net metering application assistance** — whether included and at what cost
- **Warranty terms** for panels, inverter, and workmanship

If a quotation only shows a total price with "solar system," it is not adequate. Request the breakdown — if the installer refuses, walk away.

## Step 3: Verify Equipment Quality

You do not need to be a technical expert to check the basics:

**Panels:** Ask for the datasheet of the panel model being quoted. Look for Tier-1 manufacturers (Waaree, Adani, Tata, Vikram Solar, Renewsys, and international brands like JA Solar or Longi). Check the panel's efficiency rating and warranty terms on the manufacturer's website.

**Inverter:** The inverter is the system's brain. Ask for the brand and model. Common reliable brands in India include Sungrow, Growatt, Delta, SMA, and ABB/Fimer. Check that it is compatible with your grid's voltage and has appropriate certifications (BIS, IEC).

**Mounting structure:** Should be hot-dip galvanized or aluminium to resist rust. Ask about the gauge of the structure and how it is fixed to your roof — proper waterproofing at mounting points is important.

## Step 4: Check the Payment Terms

A milestone-based payment structure protects you from non-delivery and poor workmanship:

- **10–20% on contract signing**
- **60–70% on delivery of materials to your premises**
- **Remaining 10–20% after commissioning and net metering approval**

Avoid any installer who asks for 80–100% upfront. This is the single most common pattern in solar fraud cases.

## Step 5: Ask About After-Sales Support

Solar panels last 25+ years but inverters and connections require periodic attention. Ask:

- What is the AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract) cost, and what does it cover?
- How quickly will you respond to a service call if the system stops working?
- Is monitoring (app-based or otherwise) included?
- Who handles warranty claims — you or the installer?

An installer who cannot answer these questions clearly has probably not thought through their service model.

## Step 6: Verify References

Ask for contact details of two or three customers who had similar systems installed in the last 12–18 months. Ask those customers:

- Was the installation completed on the promised timeline?
- Did any issues arise, and how were they handled?
- Is the system generating as estimated?
- Would you use this installer again?

A reputable installer will provide references without hesitation.

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Pressure to sign and pay within 24–48 hours ("subsidy deadline" urgency tactics)
- No written quotation or refusal to specify equipment models
- Full payment demanded upfront
- No mention of net metering or DISCOM coordination
- Cannot provide DISCOM empanelment proof
- No manufacturer warranty documentation offered
